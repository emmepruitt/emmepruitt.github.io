Alexis Markowski is the best big in the B1G

huskers

Author

Emme Pruitt

Published

November 6, 2022

Lincoln native Alexis Markowski was playing in Pinnacle Bank Arena in the Nebraska state tournament for Pius X High School just months before making her debut as a Husker. Being one of the most dominant posts in the area in high school, Markowski was the Nebraska Gatorade State High School Girls Basketball Player of the Year and the MaxPreps Nebraska Player of the Year.

The question was, how good would she be in college against bigger and more experienced bigs?

News flash: she is the best (and the youngest).

Code 
library(tidyverse)
library(wehoop)
library(gt)
library(cowplot)

playerbox <- load_wbb_player_box(
  seasons = 2022
) %>% separate(fg, into=c("FG", "FGA")) %>%
  separate(fg3, into=c("ThreesMade", "ThreesAttempted")) %>%
  mutate_at(3:17,as.numeric)

big <- c("Nebraska", "Iowa", "Nortwestern", "Minnesota", "Wisconsin", "Illinois", "Purdue", "Indiana", "Michigan", "Michigan State", "Ohio State", "Rutgers", "Penn State", "Maryland")

bigplayerbox <- playerbox %>%
  filter(athlete_position_abbreviation == "C" | athlete_position_abbreviation == "F") %>%
  filter(team_short_display_name %in% big) %>%
  group_by(athlete_display_name, team_short_display_name, athlete_position_abbreviation) %>%
  summarize( 
    minutes = sum(min),
    start = sum(starter),
    points = sum(pts),
    rebounds = sum(reb),
    blocks = sum(blk),
    fieldgoal = sum(FG),
    fieldgoalattempts = sum(FGA),
    threesmade = sum(ThreesMade),
    threesattempted = sum(ThreesAttempted)
    ) %>% 
  mutate(fgpct = fieldgoal/fieldgoalattempts, threepct = threesmade/threesattempted) 

alexis <- bigplayerbox %>% 
  filter(athlete_display_name == "Alexis Markowski")

bigbig <- bigplayerbox %>% 
  filter(threesattempted > 4) %>% 
  filter(fgpct > 0) %>% 
  rename(Athlete = athlete_display_name, Team= team_short_display_name) %>%
  select(Athlete, Team, fieldgoalattempts, fgpct, threesattempted, threepct) %>%
  arrange(desc(fgpct))

cumulativepoints <- playerbox %>% 
  filter(athlete_position_abbreviation == "C" | athlete_position_abbreviation == "F") %>%
  filter(team_short_display_name %in% big) %>%
  arrange(game_date) %>% 
  group_by(athlete_display_name) %>%
  mutate(TotalPoints = cumsum(pts)) 


alexiscumulative <- cumulativepoints %>% 
   filter(athlete_display_name == "Alexis Markowski")

ggplot() + 
  geom_step(data=cumulativepoints, aes(x=game_date, y=TotalPoints, group=athlete_display_name), color="gray") +
  geom_step(data=alexiscumulative, aes(x=game_date, y=TotalPoints, group=athlete_display_name), color="red", size=1.5) +
  geom_vline(xintercept=as.Date("2022-01-04"), color="dark grey") + 
  geom_text(aes(x=as.Date("2022-01-16"), y=225, label="First career start"), color="black") +
  labs(
    x="Date", 
    y="Points per game", 
    title="Where did Markowski start to shine?", 
    subtitle="The cold January weather made Alexis Markowski get hot.", 
    caption="By: Emme Pruitt  |  Source: wehoop: The SportsDataverse's R Package for Women's Basketball Data") +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(
    plot.title = element_text(size = 16, face = "bold"),
    axis.title = element_text(size = 8), 
    plot.subtitle = element_text(size=10), 
    panel.grid.minor = element_blank(),
    plot.title.position = "plot"
    )

Markowski was voted USBWA National Freshman of the Week on January 11, 2022 just one week after her standout game against No. 8 Michigan on January 4.

The true freshman’s first 20 point game of her career was against the No. 15 overall WNBA draft pick and All American Naz Hillman.

… After her first career start, it was only up from there.

But what about Nebraska? How did Markowski do in comparison to the rest of the Huskers?

The conference leader in three point percentage and the Nebraska freshman record for threes. Alexis Markowski shot 10 percent better from three than every other forward and center in the Big Ten Conference. Impressive to say the least.

Shooting 50 percent from behind the arc as a guard is incredible. The average from three is 33.3 percent. Shooting 50 percent as a center is incredible.

To answer my own question: There is no doubt that Alexis Markowski was the best in the 2021-2022 season.

Leading her team in rebounds, leading the league in three point percentage, and dominating against house hold names, Markowski was voted Big Ten Freshman of the year, and recieved eight Big Ten Freshman of the Week awards.

This year, the stakes are high for Alexis. The reigning Big Ten Freshman of the Year is on the pursuit for Big Ten Player of the year, and even Naismith Player of the Year.

The scariest part for the Big Ten and anyone in Alexis Markowski’s way is that she has three. seasons. left.
